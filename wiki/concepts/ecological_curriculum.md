# Ecological Curriculum

## Core Claim

The project's leading hypothesis is that cognition should be selected by the
ecology itself rather than by a hand-authored curriculum of explicit tasks.

## Observations From The Current Project

- Organisms operate under energy constraints, movement costs, aging, and death.
- Food distribution, terrain, spikes, and predation shape what behaviors are
  useful.
- The evaluation harness explicitly scores adaptation and competition in
  addition to viability and foraging.
- The research statement repeatedly frames ecology as part of the cognition
  problem rather than mere scenery.

## Interpretation

An ecological curriculum is attractive because it may allow increasingly rich
behavior to remain instrumentally useful as agents become more capable. In that
picture, memory, navigation, competitive behavior, and adaptive internal state
are not rewarded because the benchmark designer scripted them, but because they
help lineages survive and reproduce in a harder world.

## Failure Modes To Watch

- Ecology is too simple, so evolution finds shallow reactive hacks.
- Ecology is too harsh or noisy, so search cannot accumulate structure.
- One exploit dominates and collapses diversity.
- Benchmark metrics reward survival or score inflation without corresponding
  cognitive richness.

## Open Questions

- What ecological changes most reliably increase pressure for memory and
  navigation?
- How should nonstationarity be introduced without turning the search problem
  into noise?
- What evidence would show that ecology is driving genuine cognitive
  complexification rather than only harder optimization?
