# Ofria, Bryson, and Wilke (2009) - Avida: A Software Platform for Research in Computational Evolutionary Biology

## Summary

- Main purpose: present Avida as an experimental platform for evolutionary
  biology based on self-replicating digital organisms.
- Core claim: digital evolution can support rigorous biological inquiry because
  evolutionary history is fully observable, replayable, and experimentally
  manipulable.
- Basic mechanism: executable digital organisms reproduce by copying genomes and
  mutate through substitution, insertion, and deletion during replication.
- Resource model: replication rate can depend on computational resources and, in
  many setups, the performance of rewarded logic operations.
- Scientific value: allows full lineage tracing, repeated reruns, and direct
  testing of historical contingency.
- Major research uses reviewed in the chapter:
- Evolution of complex features such as EQU.
- Role of intermediate adaptive steps in reaching complex functions.
- Effects of mutation rate, robustness, epistasis, and quasispecies-like
  dynamics.
- Broad methodological point: Avida is framed not as a toy model but as a
  serious experimental medium for studying adaptation, complexity, and
  evolutionary causality.
