# Taylor (2019) - Routes to Open-Ended Evolution

## Summary

- Main purpose: provide a framework for analyzing different kinds of
  open-endedness rather than treating the term as a single undifferentiated
  property.
- Three kinds of open-endedness:
- Exploratory: continued search within an existing phenotypic space.
- Expansive: opening genuinely new regions of phenotypic possibility.
- Transformational: changing the space of possibilities itself through new
  entities, interactions, or organizational principles.
- Additional core framework: decompose evolutionary systems into phenotype
  generation, phenotype evaluation, and reproduction with variation.
- Important distinction: each of those processes can be implemented intrinsically
  by the evolving system or extrinsically by the designer.
- Main analytical payoff: reveals where apparent open-endedness depends on
  hidden designer scaffolding.
- Strong claim: many conventional systems can at best support exploratory
  open-endedness because their trait spaces and evaluation structures are too
  fixed.
- Historical significance: one of the clearest conceptual frameworks for
  comparing open-ended evolutionary systems.
