# Soltoggio, Stanley, and Risi (2018) - Born to Learn

## Summary

- Paper type: broad review of evolved plastic artificial neural networks
  (EPANNs).
- Core framing: intelligence in biology arises from the interaction of
  evolution, development, and lifetime learning; EPANNs aim to capture part of
  that interplay artificially.
- Main survey dimensions:
- Plasticity rules.
- Network architectures.
- Encoding schemes.
- Evolutionary setups.
- Task domains and evaluation strategies.
- Key argument: EPANNs are not just optimization tools; they are attempts to
  discover systems that can learn, adapt, recover, and sometimes inform
  hypotheses about biological intelligence.
- Major unresolved challenges identified: evaluating online learning cleanly,
  avoiding overfitting to fixed tasks, and searching efficiently over rich
  adaptive architectures.
- Historical significance: one of the clearest field-level syntheses of evolved
  plasticity research.
