# Coleman and Blair (2012) - Evolving Plastic Neural Networks for Online Learning

## Summary

- Paper type: review of evolved plastic neural networks for online learning.
- Main distinction: online learning is not the same as robustness or
  homeostasis; it requires acquiring useful new information during lifetime
  interaction.
- Core framing: evolution is used to discover architectures and plasticity rules
  that make within-lifetime adaptation possible.
- Main dimensions surveyed:
- Task domains such as navigation, adaptive control, and changing reward
  settings.
- Fitness-function design for rewarding learning rather than static competence.
- Plasticity models from Hebbian rules to reward-modulated variants.
- Encoding schemes including direct and indirect representations.
- Key caution: many systems that appear adaptive may only be tolerant to noise
  rather than genuinely capable of online learning.
- Main value: organizes the field's design space, clarifies terminology, and
  identifies unresolved evaluation and methodology problems.
