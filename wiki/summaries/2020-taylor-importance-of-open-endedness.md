# Taylor (2020) - The Importance of Open-Endedness

## Summary

- Paper type: short reply to Hintze's critique of open-endedness research.
- Main thesis: weak current definitions of open-ended evolution do not make the
  research goal itself unimportant.
- Core argument: if a simple model satisfies current criteria, that shows the
  criteria are insufficiently discriminating, not that open-endedness is a bad
  target.
- Conceptual basis: builds on Taylor's 2019 framework distinguishing
  exploratory, expansive, and transformational open-endedness.
- Important philosophical claim: open-endedness should be treated as an umbrella
  research goal covering many forms of ongoing evolutionary creativity, not one
  narrow technical condition.
- Role in the literature: defense of open-endedness as a legitimate scientific
  aim and call for better conceptual precision rather than retreat.
