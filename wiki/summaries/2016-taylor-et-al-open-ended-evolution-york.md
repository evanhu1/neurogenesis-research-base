# Taylor et al. (2016) - Open-Ended Evolution: Perspectives from the OEE Workshop in York

## Summary

- Paper type: workshop synthesis rather than a single formal theory.
- Main contribution: organizes the field of open-ended evolution into clearer
  conceptual pieces.
- Key distinction:
- Hallmarks = observable signatures such as continuing novelty, complexity
  growth, ecological diversification, and major transitions.
- Mechanisms = causal processes hypothesized to produce those hallmarks, such as
  niche construction, developmental systems, or changing selection pressures.
- Another central claim: there are multiple meaningful forms of open-endedness,
  not one simple binary criterion.
- Methodological value: prevents conflating observed novelty with proof of a
  specific underlying mechanism.
- Field-level value: catalogs benchmark systems, disagreements, themes, and open
  questions visible at the workshop.
- Historical significance: important high-level map of the conceptual terrain of
  open-ended evolution research.
