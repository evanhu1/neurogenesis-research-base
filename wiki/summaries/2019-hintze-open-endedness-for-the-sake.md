# Hintze (2019) - Open-Endedness for the Sake of Open-Endedness

## Summary

- Main claim: prevailing definitions of open-ended evolution are too weak.
- Strategy of argument: present a relatively simple computational system that
  appears to satisfy published criteria and use that as a critique of the field.
- Central criticism: indefinite novelty or ongoing activity alone is not enough
  to capture what researchers actually value in biological open-endedness.
- Main conceptual redirect: stronger attention should be paid to complexity and
  diversity, not just persistence of change.
- Important implication: a system can satisfy formal conditions for
  open-endedness while falling short of the richer intuitive target of ongoing
  meaningful innovation.
- Role in the literature: critical intervention rather than constructive system
  design roadmap.
